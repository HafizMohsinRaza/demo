The operational principle of the Gaztron’s Ammonia Cracker Unit

The Cracker Unit is a vertically-oriented rectangular furnace with a ceramic blanket (MURUGAPPA) insulation lining on the walls and top. The furnace floor is lined with refractory bricks (MAITHAN). A suspended retort made of 20%-25% Chrome/Nickel seamless tubes is positioned inside the furnace. This material is suitable for temperatures up to 1100°C, with a safe operating temperature of approximately 850°C (maximum).

The retort is centrifugally casted and has a thickness of approximately 10 mm. The furnace's side wall is equipped with coil-type Ni-chrome heating elements supported by refractory materials. The temperature of the furnace box is carefully controlled at 850°C, resulting in a catalyst bed temperature of around 800°C inside the tubes. The temperature control system utilizes twin-point temperature controllers. The lower set point is configured at 800°C to trigger an alarm in case the furnace temperature drops during normal operation. The upper set point automatically switches the heaters on and off to maintain the furnace temperature at 850°C.

The heating elements are evenly distributed throughout the height of the furnace, ensuring uniform heating of all catalyst tubes from all sides. The furnace is lined with ceramic fibers, and the maximum temperature on the outside wall of the furnace is limited to 60°C.
