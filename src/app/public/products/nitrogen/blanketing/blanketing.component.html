Nitrogen blanketing, also known as inert gas blanketing, is a highly effective method for preventing explosions and enhancing the storage stability of flammable liquids in tanks. By maintaining the oxygen content in the gas phase below 10 vol%, most gases and vapors become non-ignitable at room temperature and atmospheric pressure. This results in a secure pillow of inert gas or atmosphere above the liquid phase, creating a protective barrier.
