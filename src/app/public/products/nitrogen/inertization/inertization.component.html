Inertization is an indispensable process in industries dealing with coal and cement, as it involves creating an inert atmosphere to mitigate the risk of potentially catastrophic events. By introducing inert gases, such as nitrogen (N2), into the system, Gaztron's innovative solutions effectively displace oxygen and lower the concentration of volatile gases, significantly reducing the likelihood of explosions or fires.

The key to Gaztron's Nitrogen Inertization system lies in its seamless integration with a sophisticated process control system. In the event of a rise in CO-O2 temperature, an automatic alarm is triggered, swiftly initiating the inerting process. To ensure the utmost safety and efficiency, continuous monitoring of CO-, O2-, or temperature levels becomes paramount during the system's operation.

Under normal operation conditions, inerting commences with the introduction of the smoke from the rotary kiln plant or the hot gas generator during coal mill operations. In emergency shutdown scenarios, as well as during start-up and shutdown of the coal mill plant, inert gas injection comes into play, fortifying the system against potential hazards.

Gaztron's approach to inertization is marked by its adoption of the Blending method, commonly known as the Flushing Method, renowned for its unparalleled efficacy in suppressing fire hazards. This method involves the rapid purging of inert gas at high speed, creating a strong entry impulse into various parts of the system requiring inertization. As the gas content undergoes thorough mixing due to the induced turbulence, the optimal inerting of the system is achieved.

To attain the desired high speed, Gaztron employs specially designed nozzles, working in tandem with precisely controlled inert gas pressure at the Inerting Skid's Controlling Station. Calculations for the number and size of nozzles take into account the geometrical empty volume of the equipment that needs protection, ensuring a tailor-made and efficient inerting process.
