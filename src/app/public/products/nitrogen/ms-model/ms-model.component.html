The operational principle of Gaztron’s Nitrogen Gas Generator Plant (MS Model)

Gaztron's Nitrogen Gas Generator Plant (MS Model) operates on a sophisticated and efficient principle, making it a cutting-edge solution for on-site gas supply. The technology employed in this plant is Pressure Swing Adsorption (PSA), a revolutionary air separation technique renowned for its reliability and effectiveness.

The process begins with the passage of air through a vessel containing a highly absorbent Carbon Molecular Sieve (CMS) bed. This CMS bed possesses a unique characteristic: it exhibits a stronger affinity for oxygen compared to nitrogen. As a result, oxygen molecules are trapped within the bed, while the outgoing gas is enriched in nitrogen content. This ingenious mechanism enables the continuous production of nitrogen with exceptional purity.

To ensure uninterrupted nitrogen production, the CMS bed must be regenerated once it reaches its maximum oxygen absorption capacity. This is achieved by reducing the pressure in the vessel, liberating the absorbed oxygen, and initiating another cycle for nitrogen generation.

The PSA equipment consists of two adsorption towers, namely Tower A and Tower B, each equipped with specialized installations of the Carbon Molecular Sieve and an advanced control system. The process commences when clean compressed air enters Tower A and flows through the Carbon Molecular Sieve, where oxygen, carbon dioxide, and water are adsorbed (usually at a pressure of 0.8MPa). On the other hand, the nitrogen-rich product exits from the top of Tower A.

As the adsorption process in Tower A reaches its saturation point, it automatically halts, diverting the compressed air into Tower B. In Tower B, the oxygen adsorption and nitrogen production take place concurrently, while the regeneration process for the Carbon Molecular Sieve in Tower A is initiated. The regeneration involves releasing the trapped gases into the atmosphere, equalizing the pressure with the ambient atmospheric conditions.

Gaztron's Nitrogen Gas Generator Plant (MS Model) employs the PSA technology, which optimizes the adsorption process based on varying air pressures. By capitalizing on the distinct affinity of Carbon Molecular Sieve for different gases, the plant consistently delivers high-purity nitrogen, making it an indispensable and reliable on-site gas supply solution.

Gaztron’s Nitrogen PSA (Pressure Swing Adsorption) Technology Progression
Gaztron's Nitrogen Pressure Swing Adsorption (PSA) technology progresses through distinct stages, each carefully engineered to deliver optimal performance and consistently high-purity nitrogen. Allow us to elaborate on the intricate steps that make Gaztron's PSA technology truly exceptional:
  • Feed Air Compression and Conditioning: In this initial phase, ambient air undergoes a meticulous process of compression, executed with utmost precision by our state-of-the-art air compressor. Subsequently, the compressed air is thoroughly dried using an advanced air dryer, ensuring absolute moisture removal. Before entering the process vessels, the air is subjected to a meticulous filtration procedure, guaranteeing a clean and pristine feed for further processing.
  • Pressurization and Adsorption: Once the feed air is expertly pre-treated, it is introduced into a specialized vessel that contains the remarkable carbon molecular sieve (CMS). Within these pores, the CMS selectively adsorbs oxygen while allowing nitrogen with adjustable purity to remain in the gas stream. As the carbon molecular sieve nears its adsorption capacity, a smooth transition occurs, seamlessly shifting to another adsorber vessel, thereby ensuring continuous nitrogen separation.
  • Desorption: The liberation of the adsorbed gases is the next crucial step in our cutting-edge PSA technology. We accomplish this through a carefully orchestrated series of pressure reduction steps, facilitated by a sophisticated pressure release system. As a result of this process, a waste stream is generated, which is safely vented into the atmosphere. Consequently, the regenerated adsorbent can be reused to perpetuate the nitrogen generation process.
  • Nitrogen Receiver: At Gaztron, we recognize the significance of simultaneous adsorption and desorption processes. Consequently, our revolutionary PSA technology utilizes two adsorber vessels, one actively engaged in adsorption while the other undergoes regeneration. This arrangement ensures a consistent and uninterrupted flow of nitrogen production, guaranteeing purity levels of up to an astounding 99.995% and pressures reaching 7.5 bar (g)/ 110 PSIG.
  • Nitrogen Product: The culmination of Gaztron's Nitrogen PSA technology is an impressive and unwavering outcome—an on-site produced, high-purity nitrogen stream. Our technology's ingenuity and precision ensure that your nitrogen requirements are met with exceptional efficiency and reliability.
