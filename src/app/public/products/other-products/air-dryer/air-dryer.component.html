Types of Gaztron’s Air Dryers

1. Refrigerated Type Air Dryer

Gaztron’s refrigerated air dryer is the prevailing choice for drying compressed air and utility gases in various industrial applications, such as drying plant air, seal gas, hydrogen gas for generator cooling, and nitrogen gas drying. It is widely utilized due to its ability to maintain an acceptable dew point of +2°C at line pressure, which corresponds to a dew point of -22°C at atmospheric pressure.

2. Heatless Type Air Dryer

Gaztron’s Our heatless dryer design incorporates two vessels that are filled with desiccant, ensuring a continuous cycle that produces dry air. In this process, the wet air enters the bottom of one vessel and travels upwards through the desiccant bed, where the moisture is adsorbed. The dry air exits from the top of the vessel. Simultaneously, a small portion of the dry air is directed downward through the desiccant bed in the second vessel, which undergoes re-generation. The moisture-laden purge air is then vented out to the atmosphere, resulting in a purge loss equivalent to approximately 7.5% of the total air flow. At regular intervals, the vessels automatically switch over, ensuring a seamless transition and continuous availability of dry air without any sudden fluctuations.

3. Heat Reactivated Type Air Dryer

Heat reactivated dryers are employed when extremely low dew points of -60°C or -80°C are required for compressed air. In this particular design, the desiccant is regenerated at elevated temperatures, accompanied by a small amount of dry air purge. By utilizing dry air purging and thermal regeneration, the residual moisture loading on the desiccant is minimized, resulting in remarkably low dew points being achieved.

4. Heat of Compression (HOC) Type Air Dryer

Gaztron's Heat of Compression (HOC) Type Air Dryer offers highly efficient drying capabilities for compressed air systems. This advanced technology ensures that the compressed air is dried to an extremely low dew point, providing exceptional dryness. With the HOC design, the heat generated during the compression process is utilized for regeneration, eliminating the need for external energy sources. This not only enhances energy efficiency but also reduces operating costs.

5. No Purge Loss Type Air Dryers

Leveraging our extensive industry expertise, we provide a diverse selection of No Purge Loss Type Air Dryers. These dryers are specifically designed to eliminate water vapor from compressed air without any loss of the compressed air itself.
