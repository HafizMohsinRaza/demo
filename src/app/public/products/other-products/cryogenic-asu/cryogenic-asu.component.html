Types of cryogenic plants

Cryogenic oxygen and nitrogen plants

Cryogenic plants, designed to obtain oxygen and nitrogen, possess distinct characteristics that set them apart from other types of gas plant constructions. Here are the key points to consider:

  • Composition of Atmospheric Air: The primary components of atmospheric air are oxygen and nitrogen, with traces of other gases such as carbon dioxide, water vapor, argon, or helium.
  • Compressed Air Generation: Multi-stage compressors are used to draw in atmospheric air and compress it to obtain compressed air. Inter-coolers, moisture separators, and industrial refrigerators are employed to eliminate carbon dioxide, moisture, and hydrocarbons. This process leads to the formation of a Molecular Sieve Battery.
  • Separation of Nitrogen and Oxygen: Heat exchangers facilitate the separate paths of nitrogen and oxygen. Oxygen is compressed and directly filled into cylinders, while nitrogen requires a high-pressure compressor to complete the process.
  • Cooling and Air Routes: The purified air is then cooled using nitrogen and oxygen. It can follow two routes: a portion passes through a second heat exchanger, while another part goes through an Expansion Engine to generate additional cooling.
  • Air Release: An expansion valve is responsible for releasing the air that has undergone liquefaction.
  • Separation of Oxygen and Nitrogen: The process of separating oxygen and nitrogen involves distillation using a fractional column. This column is comprised of two sections, namely the lower and upper parts, with a condenser positioned in between. Pure nitrogen ascends to the top of the upper column, while oxygen remains at the bottom.
